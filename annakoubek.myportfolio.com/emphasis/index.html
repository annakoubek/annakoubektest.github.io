Anna Koubek

Emphasis

For this animation I drew about 300 pages on the computer. Later the drawings were put into motion.  My inspiration came from thinking about how a blind person uses their senses. These thoughts would later be put into a storyboard, resulting in animation. We split up the jobs of the animation. Meer got the first part of the animation, Amelia got the second, and I got the third. June worked on the music for the animation.
There were many challenges that I faced, one of them was communication. Since we didn't meet up outside of school, we had to talk over text or phone, which resulted in some confusion that would later be solved. (We talked about the project in school also.) Another challenge was digital animation. I had never animated digitally, and as a result it took me a long time on my first frames of my part of the animation. Since I didn't know much about digital animation, I had to figure out how to resize all of the frames because we drew the drawings on different canvas sizes.The problem with that was when I tried to resize them, the computer would always crash. I would soon later realize that the problem was with my storage space. After I figured that out, the editing of the video became less difficult.
The plot line of the animation is about a blind girl losing her ball. She goes on a journey to find it by emphasising her other senses.
In art we learned about creating emphasis through artwork. At certain parts of the animation the ball was red. This makes people notice the ball. Also emphasis was used by not drawing the background at 00:08. This makes the audience look and focus on the girl and the ball. Also we used emphasis by making the girl that appears at 00:41 wear red, but later she fades into a blue just like the people around her. This shows that she was being emphasised then deemphasized. 
In dance class, we learned that repetition and number of people affect emphasis. We used repetition for the ball bouncing to show that it was important. We made the girl walk multiple times with her stick to convey that she is blind.  When she entered the train, everyone's on their phones and they all look alike, until the girl with brown hair starts talking and says that she is going to help. Since she is the only one that is willing to help, I put emphasis on her by making her contrast with the people around her. Later on she decides that it would be better to take a selfie rather than to help the blind girl get her ball back. Dance has been integrated through the use of costumes, focus and number of people, and repetition. 
In drama, we learned that movements and costumes can change what the audience focuses on. In the animation, the girl runs into a vending machine. This is combined with dramatic music, emphasizing the scenes, especially when the beat of the music is the beat where she hits the vending machine. Another moment is when I accelerate happiness at the end with her dancing once she gets her ball back.
In creative writing, we learned that repetition is key to making things stand out. So when I was writing the actions and the storyboard, I made sure to emphasise the ball or a ball sound.  Later those things turned into an animation. I also wanted to emphasize that people don’t really look at the world around them, instead they sit on their phones, so I added the brown hair girl on the train.
In music, we learned that loud and soft music play a role in emphasis. My first example is where the girl hits the vending machine, the music is dramatic and it also matches up at that certain spot. Most of the music was created by June, but I did put some sound effects in to show more emphasis. An example would be the text message sounds. You can tell what they are just by hearing them, and since you are familiar with them, they create an emphasis of the noise. Also there is an emphasis when she falls and raises her head. The noise makes it dramatic and because her head movement is slow, it makes it the focus.
